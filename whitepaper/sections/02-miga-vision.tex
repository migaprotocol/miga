\subsection{What We Are Building}

MIGA is not another DAO. MIGA is a \textbf{Civic Operating System}---a programmable commons that can:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Start as infrastructure}: Coordination tools for diaspora
    \item \textbf{Scale toward state-like functions}: Treasury, governance, services
    \item \textbf{Avoid old failure modes}: No centralization, no capture, no corruption
    \item \textbf{Prepare for return}: When borders open, we bring home institutions
\end{enumerate}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=migagold!10,title={\textbf{The Core Innovation}}]
Separating \textbf{political governance} (one token: PARS) from \textbf{capital allocation} (ten DAO program tokens) enables specialized funding programs without fragmenting governance.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Design Principles}

\textbf{1. Consent} --- Governance derives from opt-in participation

\textbf{2. Constraint} --- Authority limited by code: bounded parameters, timelocks

\textbf{3. Transparency} --- Public execution and receipt standards

\textbf{4. Safety} --- Private voting and shielded disbursements protect humans

\textbf{5. Neutrality} --- Anti-capture: outlasts personalities and factions

\textbf{6. Pluralism} --- Multiple communities under common rules
